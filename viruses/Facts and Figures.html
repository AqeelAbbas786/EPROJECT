Facts Of Virus
Here are some detailed facts about viruses:
structure
Viruses consist of:
  • Genetic material (DNA or RNA)
  • Protein coat (capsid)
  • Lipid envelope (in some viruses)
  • Tegument layer (in some viruses)
Capsid shapes:
  • Helical (e.g., tobacco mosaic virus)
  • Icosahedral (e.g., influenza virus)
  • Complex (e.g., HIV)
  • Filamentous (e.g., Ebola virus)
Replication:
Viruses replicate inside host cells
Steps:
  1. Attachment
  2. Penetration
  3. Uncoating
  4. Transcription
  5. Translation
  6. Replication
  7. Assembly
  8. Release
Replication strategies:
  • Lytic cycle (destructive)
  • Lysogenic cycle (non-destructive)
Types:
DNA viruses:
  • Adenoviruses
  • Herpesviruses
  • Poxviruses
RNA viruses:
  • Influenza virus
  • HIV
  • Coronaviruses
Retroviruses (reverse transcriptase):
  • HIV
Prions (infectious proteins):
  • Creutzfeldt-Jakob disease
Transmission:
  • Direct contact
  • Airborne transmission
  • Vector-mediated (insects, ticks, etc.)
  • Contaminated food and water
  • Vertical transmission (mother to child)
Host range:
Viruses can infect:
  • Animals
  • Plants
  • Fungi
  • Bacteria
Specificity:
  • Some viruses infect specific species or cells
  • Others have a broad host range
Immune response:
  • Recognition by immune cells
  • Activation of immune responses
  • Production of antibodies and cytokines
  • Clearance of infected cells

Facts Of Viruses
Here are some detailed figures related to viruses:
Coronavirus
  • Acute respiratory disease
  • First detected in 2019 in Wuhan, China
  • Caused a global pandemic
  • Symptoms include fever, cough, shortness of breath, and fatigue
  • Transmitted through respiratory droplets and contact with contaminated surfaces
  • Vaccine available
Lassa Virus
  • Causes Lassa fever
  • Belongs to the Arenaviridae family
  • First described in the 1950s in Nigeria
  • 80% of infections are asymptomatic
  • Symptoms include fever, headache, muscle and joint pain, and bleeding
  • Transmitted through contact with infected rodents or their excreta
  • Outbreaks occur regularly in West Africa
Hanta Virus
  • Causes Hantavirus disease
  • Belongs to the Bunyaviridae family
  • First identified in 1993 in the US
  • Symptoms include fever, headache, muscle and joint pain, and respiratory and renal failure
  • Transmitted through contact with infected rodents or their saliva, urine, and feces
  • Outbreaks occur worldwide, especially in rural areas
Ebola Virus
  • Causes Ebola disease
  • Belongs to the Filoviridae family
  • First identified in 1976 in Sudan and Zaire
  • Symptoms include fever, vomiting, diarrhea, and bleeding
  • Transmitted through contact with infected bodily fluids, animals, or contaminated surfaces
  • Outbreaks occur in Central and West Africa
  • Vaccine available
Marburg Virus
  • Causes Marburg virus disease
  • Belongs to the Filoviridae family
  • First identified in 1967 in Marburg, Germany
  • Symptoms include fever, headache, muscle and joint pain, and bleeding
  • Transmitted through contact with infected bodily fluids, animals, or contaminated surfaces
  • Outbreaks occur in Africa
  • Vaccine available
Hepatitis Virus
  • Causes Hepatitis disease
  • Belongs to the Hepadnaviridae family
  • First identified in the 1940s
  • Symptoms include fever, fatigue, loss of appetite, nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, dark urine, and yellowing of the skin and eyes (jaundice)
  • Transmitted through contact with infected bodily fluids, contaminated food and water, and mother-to-child during childbirth
  • Outbreaks occur worldwide, especially in Asia and Africa
  • Vaccine available
